luffa (Luffa acutangula) cultivation in Sri Lanka:

Common Names and Family
Common Names: Ridge gourd, sponge gourd (Wetakolu).
Family: Cucurbitaceae.
Suitability and Distribution
Suitable Regions: Low country, mid-country wet zone, intermediate zones.
Climatic Requirements: Thrives in hot weather, can be cultivated up to 500m above sea level.
Cultivation Seasons:
Wet Zone: Throughout the year.
Dry Zone: Mainly in Maha season; can be grown under irrigation in Yala season.
Released Varieties
LA33
Asiri
Gannoruwa Ari
Soil Requirements
Well-draining sandy loam soil with organic matter.
pH range: 5.5 - 7.5.
Seed Requirement
3 kg per hectare.
Land Preparation
Harrow soil finely and mark pits with recommended spacing.
Make deep drains to prevent waterlogging.
Planting
Soak seeds in water for 24 hours.
Plant 3-4 seeds per pit, spaced apart.
Maintain adequate watering.
Spacing
Between rows and within rows: 1.5 m (1.5 m x 1.5 m).
Fertilizer Application
Organic Matter: 10 t/ha.

Basal Application (2-3 days before planting):

Urea: 75 kg/ha
TSP (Triple Super Phosphate): 195 kg/ha
MOP (Muriate of Potash): 60 kg/ha
Top Dressing One (4 weeks after planting):

Urea: 75 kg/ha
MOP: 60 kg/ha
Top Dressing Two (8 weeks after planting):

Urea: 75 kg/ha
MOP: 60 kg/ha
Water Supply / Irrigation
Twice daily irrigation until seeds germinate.
Adjust irrigation based on soil moisture.
Weed Control
Shallow root system requires careful weeding to avoid root damage.
Harvesting
Irrigate the day before harvesting.
Harvest around 14 days after flowering.
Harvest with stalk intact to preserve fruit quality.
First harvest: 60-70 days after planting.
Multiple harvests (10-15) over 1.5 - 2 months, with 4-day intervals.
Processing
Avoid direct sunlight on harvested fruits.
Pack in ventilated baskets.
Thinning Out
After 2 weeks of seedling emergence, retain two healthy plants per pit and remove others.
Training
Train vines on trellises up to 2m high.
Use vertical or horizontal trellises with ropes or wires.
Space poles 3m apart.
Remove side vines to prevent overcrowding.
Disease Management
Fungal Diseases: Powdery mildew, Downy mildew, Collar rot/Root rot, Gummy stem blight.

Management: Fungicides and sanitation practices.
Bacterial Diseases: Bacterial wilt.

Management: Soil management, crop rotation, sanitation.
Viral Diseases: Leaf Curl Virus (LCV), Papaya Ring Spot Virus (PRSV), Cucumber Mosaic Virus (CMV), Zucchini Yellow Mosaic Virus (ZYMV).

Management: Vector control, remove infected plants, use insecticides.
Phytoplasma Disease: Symptoms include stunted growth, yellowing leaves.

Transmission: Through leafhoppers, infected seeds.
Management: Remove infected plants, control leafhopper vectors.
